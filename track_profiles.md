# Residential Window Track Profiles & Interface Strategy

To ensure the "Universal Window Mount" is actually universal, it must interface with three distinct types of residential window tracks.

## 1. Vinyl (PVC) Tracks - *The Most Common*
- **Profile**: Multi-chambered, often "stepped" with built-in weep holes.
- **Key Dimension**: Rail width is typically **3mm - 5mm (1/8" - 3/16")**.
- **Interface Strategy**: Use a **Wide-Channel Gasket**. The 3D-printed panel should have a 10mm deep U-channel on the bottom edge, filled with soft EPDM foam that "swallows" the vinyl rail.

## 2. Aluminum Tracks - *Modern & Thinner*
- **Profile**: Thin, high-strength extrusions. Often features a sharp, narrow vertical "fin".
- **Key Dimension**: Fin width is typically **1.5mm - 3mm (1/16" - 1/8")**.
- **Interface Strategy**: Use a **Knife-Edge Grip**. The 3D-printed panel needs a narrower groove. A rigid plastic insert can be used to "clamp" onto the aluminum fin for extra lateral stability.

## 3. Wood Tracks - *Traditional & Deep*
- **Profile**: Flat-bottomed rectangular channels milled into the wood sill.
- **Key Dimension**: Channel width is typically **32mm - 45mm (1-1/4" - 1-3/4")**.
- **Interface Strategy**: Use a **Flat-Base Adapter**. Since there is no "fin" to grip, the mount needs a wide, flat "foot" with a thick rubber pad to create friction and prevent air bypass.

---

## Universal Seal Recommendation: The "Compression Bulb"
Regardless of the track type, the most repeatable seal is achieved by:
1.  Printing a **V-groove** around the outer perimeter of the 3D-printed panels.
2.  Inserting a standard **1/2" (12.7mm) Silicone Bulb Seal** (D-profile).
3.  Closing the window sash to compress the bulb against the frame.
